Classic Scones with jam & clotted cream

Description

A go-to british teatime treat, these scones are quick and easy to make and will go great with just about everything. Your scones should be soft and fluffy with a golden top!


    Ingredients (Makes 8):

  • 350g self-raising flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 85g butter, cubed
  • 3 tbsp caster sugar
  • 175ml milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • lemon juice
  • beaten egg, to glaze
  • jam and clotted cream (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp salt

      • Steps:

    1. Preheat the oven to 220/200c Fan/Gas 7.
    2. Pour the self-raising flour into a large bowl with 1/4 tsp salt and the baking powder, then mix.
    3. Add the butter, then rub in with your fingers until the mix looks like fine crumbs.
    4. Stir in the caster sugar
    5. Put the milk into a jug and heat in the microwave for about 30 secs until warm, but not hot. Add the vanilla extract and a squeeze of lemon juice to the milk.
    6. Put a prepare baking tray in the oven
    7. Make a well in the scone mix, then add the liquid and combine quickly with a knife until a dough is formed.
    8. Tip the dough onto a lightly floured surface, and then lightly flour your hands and the dough. Fold the dough over 2-3 times until smooth, then pat into a round about 4cm deep.
    9. Dip a 5cm round cutter into flour, plunge into the dough, then repeat until you have eight scones. You may need to squish the dough into another round to get all eight.
    10. Brush the tops with a beaten egg, then carefully arrange on the hot baking tray, and bake for 10 minutes or until risen and golden.

    I'd recommend eating them warm or cold on the day of baking, topped with jam and clotted cream (if thats your style).
